    Timeline¶

    A chronological dashboard of the 2026 ICCSD board record. Each entry is one of three kinds:

    • Board meeting: a meeting on the board calendar, linked to the meeting page on this archive.
    • Reported: a news article published by a local outlet, linked to the original story.
    • Anchor events: hand-curated pre-crisis facts and out-of-band events with source citations.

    The Timeline is auto-generated from the press index, the meeting record, and the anchor-events data file. It refreshes daily as the press file is updated. No interpretive characterization is added by the site — entries carry the source's wording (article headline, meeting title) and the source's citation; readers draw their own conclusions.

    November 2025¶

    • 2025-11 — Chief Financial Officer Adam Kurth leaves ICCSD — Adam Kurth, who had succeeded Leslie Finger as CFO in 2023, departs the district. Citations: Reported by The Cedar Rapids Gazette

    August 2025¶

    • 2025-08 — Undisclosed $10 million interfund transfer from health-insurance fund to general fund — District administrators moved $10 million from the health-insurance fund into the general fund to cover a payroll cash-flow shortfall. The transfer was not reported to the board at the time and was omitted from the next quarterly financial report. Citations: Reported by KCRG, Jan 30 2026; Detailed by The Cedar Rapids Gazette

    September 2023¶

    • 2023-09 — 2025-06 — ICCSD incurs $525,110 in federal tax penalties — Per Cedar Rapids Gazette reporting, the district accrued $525,110 in federal tax penalties between September 30, 2023 and June 30, 2025 for filing payroll and excise taxes "significantly late." Citations: The Cedar Rapids Gazette

    June 2023¶

    • 2023-06 — Former CFO Leslie Finger retires after warning successor — Cedar Rapids Gazette obtained emails showing Finger had warned his successor Adam Kurth about ~$500K in federal tax penalties, the absence of financial reports, and growth in the special-education deficit "by millions of dollars." Citations: The Cedar Rapids Gazette
