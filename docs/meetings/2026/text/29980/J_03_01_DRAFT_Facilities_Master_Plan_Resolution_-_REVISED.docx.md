Proposed Resolution on the Facilities Master Plan
(Proposed revision since April 28, 2026 Board Meeting in red)
To ensure responsible stewardship of resources, the Board of Directors of the Iowa City Community
School District directs that all Facilities Master Plan projects be paused pending a financial review.
For projects currently in the construction phase, defined as those that have received Bid Approval under
Step 7 (Policy 707.5G1), work may continue through completion of their current step; however, no
advancement to a new step shall occur without explicit Board approval.
For projects, including those currently in the planning, design, or bidding steps from Step 1 through Step
6, as well as any project identified within the Facilities Master Plan that has not yet entered Step 1 of the
District’s facilities process, the projects may complete their current step. Until the Board has adopted a
revised Facilities Master Plan, no such project shall advance to a subsequent step or enter Step 1, and no
Facilities Master Plan project negotiations or commitments shall occur. Projects currently at Step 7 or
beyond may continue toward completion through the District’s current process outlined in Policy
707.5G1.
This resolution shall remain in effect until the Board determines that the District has achieved sufficient
financial stability to resume capital projects responsibly. Financial stability to be defined as _________.
The Board will announce the determination of financial stability through a resolution. Upon
determination, the Board will direct the administration to engage the appropriate financial advisors and
bond counsel to assess the District's financial capacity and debt position, and to provide a three-year
cash flow forecast.
The Board will further direct the development of a revised Facilities Master Plan that includes a list of
prioritized projects (low, medium, and high), estimated costs, the number of students impacted, the
estimated timeline for project completion, and written justification for each project's relative
importance. The relative importance shall take into consideration, but not be limited to, issues of safety,
affordability, and the number of students impacted. Any plan must account for potential changes to the
elementary model and its capital needs. Once the Board determines the preferred projects for the
community, if any, the administration will create a funding timeline and identify funding sources. The
Board shall then vote to approve the new Facilities Master Plan through its normal methods of approval.
