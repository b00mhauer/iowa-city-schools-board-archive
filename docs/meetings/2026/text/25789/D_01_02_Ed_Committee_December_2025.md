“Rules” for paraphrasing
Rule #1: One sentence long
You are filtering what they say and offering back what’s most
important. You are not saying back everything they’ve just said
Rule #2: Start with “You” (NOT “I hear…”)
This reflects that you are leaving yourself OUT of the conversation:
it’s about the speaker, not about you.
Rule #3: Getting it wrong is just as effective as getting it right.
Paraphrasing is an offer, so it is okay to get it wrong. The speaker
will just examine it and correct you. In fact, getting it wrong often
helps people clarify their thinking.
