Prepared by and return to: Kevin D. Olson, Coralville City Attorney, 1512 7th Street, Coralville, Iowa 52241 (319) 248-1700
PUBLIC UTILITY EASEMENT
The undersigned Grantor, the Iowa City Community School District, an Iowa school
corporation, does hereby grant to MidAmerican Energy Company, Linn County Rural Electric
Cooperative, Lumen, Mediacom, South Slope Cooperative, Metronet and ImOn
Communications and their successors and assigns (hereby collectively the “Grantee”), a
perpetual easement upon, over, under, along and across the designated area the following
property owned by Grantor:
See Exhibit “A” attached hereto (the “Easement Area”).
The Grantee of this Easement shall have the right to install, lay, construct, reconstruct,
renew, operate, maintain and remove conduits, cables, pipes, and lines below the surface of the
ground, and other equipment or appurtenances above the surface of the ground as may be
necessary for the purposes of providing electric, natural gas, telephone, cable and
telecommunications services to the general public; the right to trim, cut down and remove such
trees, brush, saplings and bushes as may interfere with the proper construction, maintenance,
operation or removal of such facilities, equipment or appurtenances; and the right of ingress and
egress for all purposes aforesaid. Grantee shall promptly backfill any trench made by them, and
repair any damages caused by them within the Easement Area in a good and workmanlike
manner as nearly as possible to their original condition.
The Grantor(s) and its successors in interest and assigns, reserves the right to use said
Easement Area for purposes which will not interfere with the Grantee’s full enjoyment of the
rights hereby granted; provided that Grantors shall not erect or construct any reservoir, retaining
wall or other obstruction on said areas, or diminish or substantially add to the ground over said
Easement Area.
No permanent dwellings or trees shall be placed on the Easement Area, but, with written
advance notice authorization from Grantee’s representatives, the same may be used for gardens,
shrubs, minor landscaping or other purposes that do not then or later interfere with the aforesaid
uses or the rights herein granted. Grantee’s rights, however, included the right to remove and/or
clear away any such gardens, shrubs, minor landscaping, bushes or plantings with the Easement
Area without compensation to Grantors or its successors in interest.

Grantee shall indemnify Grantor against any loss or damage which occurs as a result of
Grantee's, or its agent’s, contractor’s, or representative’s, acts or omissions in the exercise of its easement
rights herein.
