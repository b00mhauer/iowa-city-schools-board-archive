Proposed Resolution on the Facilities Master Plan
To ensure responsible stewardship of resources, the Board of Directors of the Iowa City Community
School District directs that all Facilities Master Plan projects be paused pending a financial review.
For projects currently in the construction phase, defined as those that have received Bid Approval under
Step 7 (Policy 707.5G1), work may continue through completion of their current step; however, no
advancement to a new step shall occur without explicit Board approval.
For all other projects, including those currently in the planning, design, or bidding steps from Step 1
through Step 6, as well as any project identified within the Facilities Master Plan that has not yet entered
Step 1 of the District’s facilities process, the projects may complete their current step. Until the Board
has adopted a revised Facilities Master Plan, no such project shall advance to a subsequent step or enter
Step 1, and no Facilities Master Plan project negotiations or commitments shall occur.
This resolution shall remain in effect until the Board determines that the District has achieved sufficient
financial stability to resume capital projects responsibly. The Board will announce the determination
through a resolution. Upon determination, the Board will direct the administration to engage PFM
Financial Advisors LLC (or other appropriate financial advisors and bond counsel) to assess the District's
financial capacity and debt position.
The Board will further direct the development of a revised Facilities Master Plan that includes a
prioritized list of projects, with rankings, estimated costs, number of students impacted, and written
justification for each project's relative importance. The relative importance shall take into consideration,
but not be limited to, issues of safety, affordability, and the number of students impacted. Any plan must
account for potential changes to the elementary model and its capital needs. Once the Board determines
the preferred projects for the community, if any, the administration will create a funding timeline and
identify funding sources. The Board shall then vote to approve the new Facilities Master Plan through its
normal methods of approval.
