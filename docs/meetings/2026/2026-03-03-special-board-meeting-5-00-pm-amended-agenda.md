Two consequential financial actions: review of **Budget Reduction Proposals** (the specific cuts proposed in response to the financial crisis), and the authorization of **FY26 Anticipatory Warrants** — a short-term borrowing instrument that lets a public entity meet near-term obligations against expected future revenue.

**Public-comment context.** The use of anticipatory warrants was referenced by speakers at later meetings (including May 26) as a sign of cash-flow strain — the district was needing to borrow to bridge gaps in tax-revenue receipt timing.
